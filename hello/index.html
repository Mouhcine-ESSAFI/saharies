Answers to Your Questions

Do I need a visa to visit Morocco?

Most travelers from Europe, North America, and many other countries can enter Morocco without a visa for up to 90 days. It's advisable to check the latest requirements from the Moroccan consulate or embassy before traveling, as visa policies can change.

A visa is necessary for all visitors to Morocco, with the exception of citizens of Germany, Andorra, Algeria, Argentina, Australia, Austria, Bahrain, Belgium, Bulgaria, Brazil, Chile, Congo (Rep), Ivory Coast, Guinea, Croatia, Cyprus, Czech Republic, Denmark, Estonia, Finland, France, Great Britain, Greece, Guinea (Conakry), Hong Kong, Hungary, Iceland, Indonesia, Italy, Ivory Coast, Japan, Kuwait, Latvia, Liechtenstein, Lithuania, Luxembourg, Korea (Rep), Kuwait, Libya, Liechtenstein, Mali, Malta, Mexico, Monaco, Netherlands, New Zealand, Niger, Norway, Oman, Peru, Philippines, Poland, Puerto Rico, Qatar, Romania, Russian Federation, Saudi Arabia, Senegal, Singapore, Slovakia, Spain, Sweden, Switzerland, Tunisia, Turkey, United Arab Emirates, and Venezuela.

What is Moroccan currency? Can I use Dollars and Euros?

The currency of Morocco is the Moroccan Dirham (MAD). While major hotels and some shops in tourist areas may accept Euros and, to a lesser extent, U.S. Dollars, it is best to use Dirhams for everyday transactions. Currency exchange facilities are widely available. Outside of Morocco, several bureaus de change now provide Dirham. However, they tend to give bad conversion rates. In Morocco, you can obtain Dirhams at “bureau de change” counters in airports, major banks, and some hotels, as well as through ATMs, which are prevalent in major towns and cities.

ATMs typically offer the best exchange rates and accept most international credit and debit cards. However, it's important to note that ATMs can be less common in rural areas, and credit cards might be refused. Before traveling, inform your bank of your plans to use your card abroad to prevent any blocks on your card for fraud prevention. While traveler’s checks can be useful in emergencies, cashing them may be time-consuming, and they are becoming less accepted. For returning excess Dirhams, ensure you retain your currency exchange slips for the transaction.

Is Morocco a safe country?

Indeed, Morocco is a safe destination for travelers. As a visitor, it's unlikely you'll face any serious harm as only minor crimes (scams and pickpocketing) are common. Presently, Morocco is extremely secure for travelers.

How much does a trip to Morocco cost?

Travel expenses in Morocco can differ greatly depending on your choices of travel style and accommodations. Typically, a solo traveler may spend around $1,259 for a seven-day journey, a couple about $1,860, and a family of four roughly $2,640. Such a trip often covers various cities including Marrakech, Merzouga, Fes, and Chefchaouen. Accommodation costs in Morocco are quite diverse, catering to a broad range of preferences and budgets.

When should I visit the desert in Morocco?

The ideal time to visit Morocco is in spring (March to May) or fall (September to November), when mild, pleasant temperatures make daytime exploration, especially desert excursions, enjoyable. Summers can be extremely hot in the southern regions, and winter nights can get quite cold. However, regardless of the season, you'll always find regions with suitable weather.

Where should I go for the first time in Morocco?

First-time visitors shouldn't miss the highlights of Marrakech, Merzouga, Chefchaouen, and Fes. Each city offers a unique blend of history, desert adventures, cultural experiences, delicious cuisine, and stunning architecture.

Can I drink tap water in Morocco?

While tap water in Morocco is generally considered safe for local residents, We recommend that you opt for bottled water. Because your body may not be accustomed to the different mineral content and microorganisms in the water. To avoid discomfort or illness you should drink bottled water. It is widely available and ensures a safer option for those unaccustomed to the local water supply.

How to dress in Morocco as a female traveller?

Morocco is a Muslim country, so it's respectful to dress modestly. Loose-fitting clothes are both culturally appropriate and comfortable in the Moroccan climate.

Is Wifi widely available in Morocco?

Wifi is widely available in major cities and tourist areas. Many hotels, restaurants, and cafes offering this service for their clients during their stay. However, connectivity can be less reliable in rural or remote areas.

Can I bring a drone to Morocco?

The use of drones is heavily regulated in Morocco, and it's illegal to import or use drones without specific authorization from the Moroccan authorities. Violation of this law can result in fines or confiscation of the drone at the airport.

What if I have dietary restrictions when traveling in Morocco?

If you have dietary restrictions, you'll find that many Moroccan restaurants and markets can accommodate various needs. Vegetarian and vegan versions of famous Moroccan dishes like couscous, tagines, and salads are widely available. Gluten-free diners should inquire carefully about ingredients, as wheat is often used. It's helpful to carry a card in French or Arabic explaining your specific dietary needs to ensure clear communication. Larger cities and tourist areas usually offer more diverse food options, but in rural regions, it may be best to enquire before hands.
